# PPO Policy Evaluation

`PPO_subset/` evaluates a Proximal Policy Optimization ego policy under the same
long-tail scenario generator used by the IDM baseline. The adversary trajectory is
generated by the frozen conditional diffusion model, while the ego vehicle is
controlled by a stable-baselines3 PPO checkpoint:

```text
PPO_subset/weights/ppo/model.zip
```

The PPO policy is not trained during evaluation. It is loaded once and used for
deterministic inference by default.

## Policy Interface

The ego policy receives a 5x5 kinematics observation:

```text
[presence, x, y, vx, vy] for ego, target, and nearest vehicles
```

It outputs one of the original highway-env discrete MDP actions:

```text
LANE_LEFT, IDLE, LANE_RIGHT, FASTER, SLOWER
```

Thus the PPO policy is allowed to change lanes where the environment has available
lanes. The environment geometry is kept fixed by the experiment configuration:
following is single-lane and cut-in is two-lane.

## Comparable Evaluation

All non-policy components are shared with the IDM baseline:

```text
same highD tail scenario-condition distribution
same deterministic DDIM adversary sampler
same initial-state reconstruction
same EVT/GPD safety-critical threshold
same subset-simulation estimator
same final-level playback mechanism
```

The compressed result JSON records fairness checks against IDM, including the EVT
threshold, subset sample count, and `p0`.
